Smart Fat Loss Without
Losing Muscle

Priya Nair Nutrition & Body Composition Coach

The Calorie Deficit: Getting It Right

Fat loss fundamentally requires a calorie deficit — consuming fewer calories than your body burns. However, the size of that deficit matters enormously. Too aggressive a cut (more than 500–700 kcal below maintenance) accelerates muscle loss, tanks energy levels, and makes the diet unsustainable.

A moderate deficit of 300–500 kcal per day allows for steady fat loss of roughly 0.5kg per week while giving your body enough fuel to preserve lean tissue.

"Crash diets burn muscle. Smart deficits burn fat. The difference is patience."

Protein: Your Muscle's Best Defense

When calories drop, protein becomes even more critical. High protein intake during a cut serves two purposes: it preserves muscle mass by providing the amino acids needed for repair, and it keeps you fuller for longer, making adherence easier.

Aim for 2.0–2.4g of protein per kilogram of body weight during a fat loss phase — slightly higher than during a muscle-building phase to counteract the muscle-sparing challenge of a deficit.

Best Protein Sources for a Cut

  • Chicken breast, turkey, and lean beef
  • Eggs and egg whites
  • Low-fat Greek yogurt and cottage cheese
  • White fish: tilapia, cod, and haddock
  • Plant-based: tofu, tempeh, and lentils

Strength Training: Non-Negotiable

The biggest mistake people make during fat loss phases is switching to exclusively cardio. Resistance training sends a clear signal to your body: this muscle is needed, don't break it down. Even two to three strength sessions per week during a cut dramatically reduces muscle loss compared to cardio-only approaches.

Reduce volume slightly if needed for recovery, but maintain intensity. Keep lifting as heavy as you can manage — this is what tells your body muscle is worth keeping.

Cardio: A Tool, Not a Punishment

Cardio accelerates the calorie deficit without reducing food intake further. Low-intensity steady-state (LISS) cardio — such as brisk walking or cycling — is ideal during a cut because it burns calories without significantly taxing recovery.

  1. 3–5 sessions per week of 30–45 minutes LISS cardio
  2. Keep 1–2 HIIT sessions for cardiovascular fitness
  3. Walk more throughout the day — NEAT adds up fast
  4. Avoid excessive cardio that eats into recovery capacity
  5. Schedule cardio away from your heaviest lifting days
